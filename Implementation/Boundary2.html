5.7. Application of Boundary Conditions¶

5.7.1. Overview¶

After assembly, the global system of equations \([K]\{u\}=\{F\}\) that describe the finite element problem is singular. Meaning that, the equations for the unknown degrees of freedom are non-invertible. This situation arises when there are no solutions or infinite solutions to a problem. Boundary conditions must be applied to make the system non-singular.

Boundary conditions are applied by

  • modifying the global stiffness and force arrays on Dirichlet boundaries, and
  • modifying the global force on Neummann boundaries.

The method shown adopts the same assumptions as the assembler in Assembly, Part I.

5.7.2. Example¶

Consider the problem of determining the displacements along the tapered bar shown

Tapered bar discretized by four spring elements.

The assembled global equations are given by

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} k_{1} & -k_{1} & 0 & 0 & \\ -k_{1} & k_{1} + k_{2} & -k_{2} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -k_{2} & k_{2} + k_{3} & -k_{3} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -k_{3} & k_{3} + k_{4} & -k_{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -k_{4} & k_{4} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3} \\ u_{4} \\ u_{5} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{Bmatrix}\end{split}\]

where \(k_e=A_eE_e/h_e\) is the stiffness coefficient of the e\(^{\rm th}\) element. The boundary conditons are

\[u_{x=0}=\overline{u}_1, \quad \left[AE\frac{du}{dx}\right]_{x=L}=F\]

Substituting in to gives

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} k_{1} & -k_{1} & 0 & 0 & \\ -k_{1} & k_{1} + k_{2} & -k_{2} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -k_{2} & k_{2} + k_{3} & -k_{3} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -k_{3} & k_{3} + k_{4} & -k_{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -k_{4} & k_{4} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} \overline{u}_1 \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3} \\ u_{4} \\ u_{5} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} R \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ F \\ \end{Bmatrix}\end{split}\]

where \(R\) is an unkown reaction at node 1. The displacements \(u_{2}, \ u_{3},\) and \(u_{4}\) do not explicitly depend on \(R\), allowing to be written as

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -k_{1} & k_{1} + k_{2} & -k_{2} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -k_{2} & k_{2} + k_{3} & -k_{3} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -k_{3} & k_{3} + k_{4} & -k_{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -k_{4} & k_{4} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3} \\ u_{4} \\ u_{5} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} \overline{u}_1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ F \\ \end{Bmatrix}\end{split}\]

The global system can now be solved for the unknown displacements and the unknown reaction can then be determined as \(R=-k_1\overline{u}_1 + \left(k_1+k_2\right)u_2\). However, solving has the disadvantange that it is no longer a symmetric system due to the way the boundary conditions were applied. Symmetry of the global equation can be easily recovered by adding \(\begin{bmatrix}0 & k_1\overline{u}_1 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}^T\) to both sides of , giving

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & k_{1} + k_{2} & -k_{2} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -k_{2} & k_{2} + k_{3} & -k_{3} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -k_{3} & k_{3} + k_{4} & -k_{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -k_{4} & k_{4} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3} \\ u_{4} \\ u_{5} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} \overline{u}_1 \\ k_{1}\overline{u}_1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ F \\ \end{Bmatrix}\end{split}\]

In general, for known DOF \(\overline{u}_i\), the global stiffness and force arrays are modified according to

\[\begin{split}K_{ij} = K_{ji} = \delta_{ij}, \quad F_j = \begin{cases} \overline{u}_i & i = j \\ F_j-K_{ji}\overline{u}_i & i \ne j \end{cases}\end{split}\]

where \(\delta_{ij}\) is the Kronecker delta.

5.7.3. Computational Implementation¶

In pyfem2, application of the boundary conditions is performed in the method FiniteElementModel.apply_bc_udof. (udof stands for “uniform degree of freedom”).

A representative python code that applies boundary conditions is the function ApplyBoundaryConditions, shown below. ApplyBoundaryConditions is invoked as

Kbc, Fbc = ApplyBoundaryConditions(K, F, doftags, dofvals)

The arguments ApplyBoundaryConditions have been described in Assembly, Part I.

The output is:

Kbc Boundary condition modified global stiffness
Fbc Boundary condition modified global force 
def ApplyBoundaryConditions(K, F, doftags, dofvals):

    N, m = doftags.shape
    Kbc, Fbc = K.copy(), F.copy()

    # Dirichlet boundary conditions
    for i in range(N):
        for j in range(m):
            if doftags[i,j] == 0:
                I = i * m + j
                Fbc -= [K[k,I] * dofvals[i,j] for k in range(N*m)]
                Kbc[I, :] = Kbc[:, I] = 0
                Kbc[I, I] = 1

    # Further modify RHS for Dirichlet boundary
    # This must be done after the loop above.
    for i in range(N):
        for j in range(m):
            if doftags[i,j] == 0:
                Fbc[i*m+j] = dofvals[i,j]

    return Kbc, Fbc
