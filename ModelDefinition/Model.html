3.1. The finite element model¶

3.1.1. Overview¶

A finite element model

  • is an object that stores model and history and data;
  • assembles and solves the linear system representing a physical system; and
  • is derived from the FiniteElementModel base class.

3.1.2. Finite element model classes¶

3.1.2.1. N-dimensional truss¶

A truss model is built from two-node elastic bar elements. In two dimensions, a system made up of elastic bar elements is referred to as a plane truss. In three dimensions, the system is referred to as a space truss.

V = TrussModel(mesh)

Applicable elements¶

  • ElasticLink1D2
  • ElasticLink2D2
  • ElasticLink3D2

Active degrees of freedom¶

  • X, Y (two-dimensions), Z (three-dimensions)

Valid degree of freedom assignments¶

  • Prescribing degrees of freedom
  • Fixing displacement degrees of freedom
  • Pinning displacement degrees of freedom

Valid loading conditions¶

  • Concentrated loads

3.1.2.2. Plane beam-column truss¶

The two-dimensional plane beam-column truss model is built from combinations of two-node elastic bar and Euler-Bernouli beam elements.

V = PlaneBeamColumnTrussModel(mesh)

Applicable elements¶

  • ElasticLink2D2
  • BeamColumn2D

Active degrees of freedom¶

  • X, Y, TZ

Valid degree of freedom assignments¶

  • Fixing displacement degrees of freedom
  • Pinning displacement degrees of freedom
  • Prescribing degrees of freedom

Valid loading conditions¶

  • Concentrated loads

3.1.2.3. Plane elasticity¶

Two-dimensional plane elasticity, in the form of plane stress and plane strain, is represented by the Plane2DModel object:

V = Plane2DModel(mesh)

Applicable elements¶

  • PlaneStrainQuad4
  • PlaneStrainQuad4Reduced
  • PlaneStressQuad4
  • PlaneStrainQuad8
  • PlaneStressQuad8

Active degrees of freedom¶

  • X, Y

Valid degree of freedom assignments¶

  • Fixing displacement degrees of freedom
  • Pinning displacement degrees of freedom
  • Prescribing degrees of freedom

Valid loading conditions¶

  • Concentrated loads
  • Distributed load
  • Surface load vector
  • Normal surface load vector
  • Gravity loads
  • Pressure

3.1.3. Two-dimensional diffusive heat transfer¶

Two-dimensional diffusive heat transfer is modeled by the HeatTransfer2DModel object:

V = HeatTransfer2DModel(mesh)

3.1.3.1. Applicable elements¶

  • DiffusiveHeatTransfer2D3

3.1.3.2. Active degrees of freedom¶

  • T

3.1.3.3. Valid degree of freedom assignments¶

  • Initial temperature
  • Prescribing degrees of freedom

3.1.3.4. Valid loading conditions¶

  • Surface flux
  • Surface convection
  • Heat generation
